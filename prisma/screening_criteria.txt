INCLUSION CRITERIA:
- Study focuses on Communication Network Theory (CNT) or communication networks from a social science perspective
- Study addresses theoretical critiques of CNT regarding agency, meaning-making, or temporal dynamics
- Study discusses structural determinism, semantic/meaning issues, or dynamic network processes in communication contexts
- Study examines the "dark side" of networks (negative ties, collaborative drag, power dynamics, surveillance)
- Study proposes theoretical innovations to CNT (e.g., CCO, semantic network analysis, dynamic network analysis, multiplex networks)
- Study is empirical research or theoretical/conceptual work published in peer-reviewed venues (2015-2025)
- Study is accessible in English and contains sufficient methodological or theoretical detail

EXCLUSION CRITERIA:
- Study focuses purely on technical/computer networks without communication theory perspective
- Study is about social network analysis in domains other than communication (e.g., pure epidemiology, biology)
- Study treats networks only as methodological tools without theoretical engagement
- Study is a book review, editorial, conference abstract, or news article
- Study predates 2015 (outside review period)
- Study lacks theoretical depth or is purely descriptive
- Study is not in English or full text is not accessible
