# Preliminary Findings: Indonesian Disinformation Landscape (H1 2026)

Based on the analysis of **3,989 fact-checked hoaxes** from January 1 to June 30, 2026, the following three core themes have emerged. These empirical findings provide a strong basis for a Communication Studies research paper.

---

## 1. The "Public-to-Private" Disinformation Pipeline (Economy of Despair)

### The Empirical Data
**Total Scam/Aid Hoaxes identified:** 1,039 (Labeled + Keyword Inferred)

**Top Targeted Brands/Keywords:**
* Tidak (170) | Benar (168) | Video (105)
* Hibah (91) | Bansos (90) | Umumkan (85)
* Juta (80) | Bank (48) | Usaha (44) | Modal (42) | Subsidi (40)

**Origin Platforms (Source URLs):**
1. `web.facebook.com`: 74 sources
2. `archive.ph`: 67 sources
3. `facebook.com`: 66 sources
4. `tiktok.com`: 19 sources

**Spread Vectors (Mentions in Fact-Check Narratives):**
1. **WhatsApp (Dark Social):** 2,791 mentions
2. **Instagram:** 1,473 mentions
3. **Facebook (Public Social):** 1,196 mentions
4. **TikTok:** 634 mentions

### The Finding
Financial scams (*Bansos*, *Hibah*, *Modal Usaha*) make up a massive chunk of the hoax landscape (over 1,000 cases). However, the data reveals a distinct two-step flow of communication. The hoaxes overwhelmingly originate on public, legacy social platforms (Facebook accounts for the vast majority of source URLs), but they achieve virality through "Dark Social" networks (WhatsApp was mentioned 2,791 times, nearly double Instagram or Facebook).

### Academic Significance
This shows that scammers use Facebook as a "hosting" ground, but rely on the intimate, high-trust, and encrypted environment of WhatsApp family and community groups to actually extract money and data from vulnerable people experiencing economic hardship.

## 2. The "Glocalization" of Indonesian Political Disinformation

### The Empirical Data
**Total Political Hoaxes identified:** 1,465 (Labeled + Keyword Inferred)

**Specific Political Actors & Geopolitics:**
* **[Intl]** Middle East (Iran/Israel/Gaza): 569 hoaxes
* **[Domestic]** Prabowo Subianto: 285 hoaxes
* **[Intl]** US Politics (Trump/Biden): 178 hoaxes
* **[Domestic]** Joko Widodo: 141 hoaxes
* **[Domestic]** Gibran R.: 98 hoaxes
* **[Intl]** China: 76 hoaxes
* **[Domestic]** Demonstrations / Students: 42 hoaxes
* **[Domestic]** Corruption / KPK: 28 hoaxes
* **[Domestic]** Free Nutritious Meals (MBG): 1 hoax

### The Finding
While one might assume domestic politics (Prabowo's new presidency, student protests) would dominate political hoaxes in 2026, the data shows that **International Geopolitics completely dwarfs domestic issues**. There were nearly double the amount of hoaxes about the Middle East and US Politics compared to domestic attacks on Prabowo or Jokowi. Specific domestic policies, like the Free Nutritious Meals (MBG) program, barely register.

### Academic Significance
Indonesian political discourse has become highly "glocalized." Disinformation actors are tapping into the high emotional resonance of global conflicts to generate engagement, rather than criticizing domestic governance.

## 3. The "Anti-Disinformation Paradox" (Empirical Reality vs. State Narrative)

### The Empirical Data
**Total Hoaxes analyzed:** 3,989

**True Hoax Categories (After Keyword Inference):**
1. **Lain-lain / Pop Culture:** 1,330 (33.3%)
2. **Politik (Total):** 1,332 (33.4%) *(872 Inferred + 460 Labeled)*
3. **Ekonomi/Scam (Total):** 840 (21.1%) *(627 Inferred + 134 Lowongan + 79 Bantuan)*
4. **Kesehatan:** 324 (8.1%)

**Origin Domains of Disinformation (Parsed from Original URLs):**
* `web.facebook.com`: 219 (27.2%)
* `archive.ph` & `archive.today`: 201 (24.9%)
* `facebook.com`: 145 (18.0%)
* `tiktok.com` & `vt.tiktok.com`: 72 (9.0%)

### The Finding
The Indonesian government has been pushing a controversial "Anti-Disinformation" bill under the guise of protecting the state from "foreign propaganda" and high-level information warfare. However, this empirical data disproves that justification. When recategorized, the landscape is not dominated by sophisticated foreign subversion. Instead, over 20% of all hoaxes are mundane domestic financial scams, and the political hoaxes are largely organic, sensationalist clickbait about global events spreading on Facebook and TikTok.

### Academic Significance & Conclusion
You can argue that the government's securitization of "hoaxes" is a framing tactic. By pointing to the empirical reality—that the disinformation ecosystem is mostly low-level scams and organic geopolitical chatter—you can argue that draconian new speech laws are a disproportionate response designed more for state control than actual media hygiene.
